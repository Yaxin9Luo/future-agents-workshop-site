Why this workshop

Agents are leaving the benchmark box.

The next generation of AI systems will browse, code, call tools, manage memory, coordinate with people, and act over long horizons. The field needs a sharper conversation about the future of this paradigm and the limits that remain when agents meet real tasks.

We want this workshop to be a meeting point for ambitious systems work and skeptical measurement. Strong agents should be paired with strong failure analysis: autonomy boundaries, escalation policies, robustness, human oversight, compute cost, privacy, and evaluation protocols that survive contact with real workflows.

01
From tasks to trajectories How should we measure agents that plan, recover, delegate, and learn from interaction rather than answer once?
02
From capability to control Where should agents be allowed to act, when should they ask, and how should systems expose uncertainty?
03
From demos to evidence What experimental standards make agent claims reproducible across environments, users, and time?

Scope

Core questions for agent research.

We welcome technical papers, empirical studies, position papers, datasets, benchmarks, tools, and deployed-system analyses that make agentic systems more capable, more measurable, or more honest about their limits.

Long-horizon reliability

Planning, memory, recovery, self-correction, and failure modes over multi-step tasks.

Computer and tool use

Agents operating browsers, terminals, IDEs, APIs, simulations, robots, and scientific instruments.

Evaluation protocols

Benchmarks, human studies, automatic judging, contamination control, and reproducibility.

Limits of autonomy

Escalation, uncertainty, intervention, safe fallback, delegation, and human-agent collaboration.

Learning from interaction

Memory updates, preference learning, environment feedback, online adaptation, and continual improvement.

Security and governance

Prompt injection, tool misuse, privacy, audit trails, authorization, monitoring, and deployment policy.

Submission

Call for Papers

Formal page limits, formatting rules, anonymity policy, and the submission portal will be updated after the NeurIPS 2026 workshop process is finalized.

We are especially interested in work that connects engineering detail with principled evaluation. Agent research often moves quickly from impressive demos to broad claims; this workshop will privilege papers that make those claims testable.

Expected formats include technical papers, empirical studies, benchmark or dataset papers, systems papers, and position papers. We will also welcome rigorous negative results and limitation analyses.

Submission Portal TBA Accepted Papers
  • Methods: new agent architectures, memory systems, planning loops, verification layers, and tool-use mechanisms.
  • Evaluations: benchmarks, protocols, datasets, judges, and analysis methods for real-world agent behavior.
  • Systems: deployed agents, research infrastructure, sandboxing, monitoring, and reproducible harnesses.
  • Limitations: negative results, failure taxonomies, reliability studies, safety analysis, and governance proposals.
